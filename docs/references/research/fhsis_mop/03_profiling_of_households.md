Chapter 3 
Profiling of Households 
 
A. Definition  
 
Household Profiling  is an essential process of listing every household in a given 
barangay and obtaining the names of each member of the household with their b asic 
characteristics and information. This process is expected to facilitate the identification 
of clients to be targeted for delivering package of health care services. 
 
Household as defined by the Philippine Statistical Authority (PSA) is a social unit 
consisting of a person living alone or a group of persons who sleep in the same 
housing unit and have a common arrangement in the preparation and consumption of 
food. 
 
B. Purpose of HH Profiling 
 
Profiling of households in a given barangay is an essential process that will facilitate 
the identification of clients targeted to receive the necessary package of health care 
and services. This entails a house to house visit by the midwife with assistance from 
the c ommunity-based volunteer health workers (BHW s, BNS s, BSPO s) including 
nurses under the DOH Nurse Deployment Program (NDP) . They need to list every 
individual member of the household, and ask them for basic information for the 
following purposes:  
 
(1) To obtain an overview of the household you are servi ng in terms of 
demographics;  
 
(2) Identify members who need further assessment of their health risks and 
needs. 
 
C. Information to be Obtained from Each Household 
 
 Basic Information. These include general information about the household, 
name of the respondent, socio -economic profile whether they are NHTS, 
particularly if they are beneficiary of the 4Ps Program or not and if they are 
indigenous people including their PhilHealth membership status. 
 
 Demographic and Health Profiles. These include the names of all members 
of the Household including their relationship to the household head, sex, ages, 
birthdays, out of school, and the specific classification of household members 
by age and the health conditions.

D. Process in Household Profiling 
 
It is expected that each HH in the catchment barangay has been assigned to a 
specific BHW or other community volunteer worker. Ideally, each BHW should cover 
about 20-25 HHs. However, this is not usually followed especially if there are only a 
few BHWs in the barangay. Other volunteer workers or health staff (e.g. 
NDPs/RHMPP”s) are also mobilized to handle specific HHs in a given barangay. As a 
process, the HH profiling entails the following steps: 
 
1. HH profiling must be conducted and completed at the beginning of the year 
(January), and must be updated on a quarterly basis. HH Profiles must be 
completed/ updated at the end of the first month of each quarter. 
 
2. The designated BHW/community volunteer w orker/NDP shall conduct the 
profiling of HHs assigned to her by the midwife. The MW must ensure that all 
HHs in her catchment barangay has been assigned to a specific 
BHW/community volunteer worker/NDP;  
 
3. All accomplished HH profiles will be submitted to t heir supervising midwives 
not later than the third week of  January (and the  first months of every quarter 
for the updated profiles); 
 
4. The MW supervisor will compile all completed HH profiles to become the basis 
for master listing the different categories of clients to be reached/served; 
 
5. The BHWs/community volunteer worker/NDP shall make use of the HH 
Profiling Form and be guided by the following steps: 
 
5.1 Ask permission to take the profile of the household.  
  Ask permission that you will first get the pro file of the household.  
Explain briefly that you will be asking some questions and hope that 
the respondent provides the info as completely as possible. 
 
5.2 Accomplish the Basic Information. 
Ensure that the respondent is the head of the household, spouse, or 
any member of the family who is primarily responsible for making 
decisions about the health of the household.  Fill out the rest of the 
Basic Information fields according to the prescribed format.   
 
5.3 List all the members of the household, indicating each one’s 
relationship to the household head, sex, age, and birthday. 
Include ALL the members of the household. Start with the household 
head, and followed by the spouse/partner, their children  from eldest to 
youngest, and lastly, other members of the family.

5.4 Obtain the birthdate  of each member . Write the birthdates of each 
member under the appropriate column. For those who do not know 
their birthdays or exact age, help them to estimate their age. 
 
5.5 Classify each member according to the following age -group or 
health-risk group. Use the following c odes to classify each member 
every quarter. 
 
N - Newborn for those who are 0-28 days old 
I - Infant for those who are 29 days old-11months old 
U - Under-five child for those who are 1 -4 years old or 12 -59 
months old 
S - School-Aged Children for those who are 5-9 years old 
A - Adolescents for those who are 10-19 years old 
P - Any pregnant woman even if she is <15 or >49 years old 
AP - Adolescent and Pregnant at the same time 
PP - Post-Partum Woman for those who have given birth in  last 
6 weeks, even if she is < 15 or >49 years old) 
WRA - Women of Reproductive Age for those who are 15 -49 
years old who are not currently pregnant or post-partum 
SC - Senior Citizens for those who are 60 years old and above 
PWD - Persons with Disability 
AB - Adult 25-59 y/o 
 
5.6 Validate PhilHealth membership. Ask the respondent if the HH Head 
is a member of PhilHealth. Preferably, ask for the HH Head’s 
PhilHealth ID Card and note the ID number in the appropriate column. 
Then, check the PhilHealth  membership of all the other adults in the 
household (those who are 21 years old and above).   
 
5.7 Check completeness of entries. Before leaving the house, check if 
all entries have been accomplished. Thank the respondent and 
encourage them to visit the healt h facility for any health concern or 
need. 
 
5.8 Submit the ac complished form to the Midwife.  Submit the 
completed form to the midwife. For HHs who were not covered during 
your first round of visit, you need to schedule and conduct the profiling 
before the third week of January or first month of every quarter.  
 
5.9 Midwife to maintain and  keep accomplished HH profiles.  The 
supervising midwife should review the completeness of the entries of 
each HH profile submitted. She needs to compile the accomplished 
forms and use it as reference for preparing the Master Lists/TCL for 
each of Age-Group or health risk group. 
 
5.10 Update the HH profiles every first month of the subsequent 
quarters.

The midwife shall mobilize the assigned BHWs/community volunteer 
workers/NDP to update the HH profiles every quarter.  Using the same 
accomplished HH Profile, the BHWs/community volunteer 
workers/NDPs shall conduct another round of home visits to their 
assigned HHs to make the necessary update. Updates will be focused 
mainly on the following:  
 
(1) Changes in the classification of HH members.  For example, the 
newborn in the last quarter has already to be classified as infants, 
WRA who were not pregnant during the last profiling may already 
be pregnant in the next round of visit, etc. 
 
(2) Transfer of residences. It is possible that some HHs may no longer 
be residing in the same barangay and transferred to another area. 
Place this under Remarks Column. 
 
(3) New Residents. It may also be possible that a new HH has arrived 
in the barangay. They should likewise be profiled. 
 
E. Tool in HH Profiling 
 
The Household Profile Form will be your main tool for the HH Profiling. This 
has two main parts: 
 
Part 1.  Records the Basic Information of the HHs including the date of the HH 
visit and name of Respondents. 
 
Part 2. Lists all the names of the individual members of the HH beginning with 
the HH head, spouse, children, children with their spouses and the 
grandchildren including extended family members and HH help. 
  
Reminder: 
1. LGUs may have already established their own HH Profiling Form. 
As such, there is no need for them to accomplish this HH Profile 
Form as long as these are updated and provide the essential data 
needed for the proper Master Listing of Clients targe ted to be 
provided with health care and services.  
2. LGUs may bring with them the other masterlists of other 
programs (e.g. Masterlist of Households on Environmental Health 
and Sanitation, Prenatal Care and  Delivery Outcome, Masterlist of 
Women of Reproductive Age, etc.)
