Chapter 9.1 
Data Validation 
 
A. Importance and Principles of Data Validation 
 
Accuracy of data is an inherent element in the overall monitoring and evaluation 
system. For data/information to be useful and relevant, these must be accurate in the 
light of their definition, the means and terms of standard measurements as we ll as in 
their presentation. Validation of the accuracy of data is an essential process in 
monitoring and evaluation to ensure that any decision or action to be undertaken as a 
result are really based on facts. Inaccurate and incomplete information leads to wrong 
interpretation and ultimately results to wrong decisions and actions. As the saying 
goes, garbage in and garbage out.  
 
Data validation of must be inherent in every step of the FHSIS process from data 
collection, to recording, to consolidation and to submission  at various levels of 
administration. Validation must happen right at the time the data are recorded and 
consolidated before these are submitted to the next level. The BHS midwives who 
initially record and consolidate data on program accomplis hment should be able to 
double check their accuracy prior to submitting her report to the next level. On the 
other hand, the recipient/s and users of data must also be able to check on a timely 
basis the accuracy and completeness of the information they re ceive. More 
importantly, results of data validation must be fed -back immediately to the source of 
the data or report for immediate reckoning of data. 
 
B. Data Validation Schemes 
 
There are different ways to validate the accuracy and completeness of FHSIS data  
from as simple as checking on the totals or sums of counts to as complex as checking 
the accuracy of data according to the definition and formula given each indicator . The 
following describes these different schemes.

1.4 Validating Proportion/Percentages 
 
Child Care 
 
Infants ages 6 -11 months given Vitamin A and Children ages 12 - 59 months 
given Vitamin A should not be higher than the eligible population. Otherwise, 
infants ages 6 -11 months and children ages 12 -59 months Given Vitamin A 
will result to a percentage of more than 100% of the eligible population. 
 
Environmental Health 
Households with sanitary disposal of Solid waste and Households with 
complete basic sanitation facilities should not be higher than Total number of 
Households. 
 
Validate all percentage values that are higher than 100%.
