Chapter 8.2 
Recording and Reporting  
Mortality and Natality Data 
  
 
A. Definition and Classifications 
 
Mortality is another term for death. Mortality is usually expressed as a rate  with the 
number of deaths in a given p opulation over a given period of time  (by dividing the 
total number of deaths by the total population multiplied by 1,000 ). Mortality rates can 
be due to a specific disease or groups of diseases, and also according to  a specific 
age group. The FHSIS records and reports the following mortality data:   
 
 Maternal Mortality Ratio.  The number of maternal deaths in a given period over the 
total number of live births over the same period multiplied by 100,000. Maternal deaths  
are due to pregnancy, childbirth and puerperium. 
 
 Under-five Mortality Rate. The number of deaths among under -five children (from birth 
up to 59 months of age) among the total number of live births multiplied by 1,000 
 
 Infant Mortality Rate. The number of deaths among infants (0-11 months and 29 days 
old) among the total number of live births multiplied by 1,000 
 
 Neonatal Mortality Rate . The number of deaths among newborns (0 -28 days old) 
among the total number of live births multiplied by 1,000 
 
 Perinatal Mortality Rate. The number of fetal deaths( deaths of fetuses weighing at 
least 500g or, when birth weight is unavailable after 22 completed weeks age of 
gestation or with a crown –heel length of 25 cm or more PLUS the number of early 
neonatal deaths (0-6 days old) among the total number of live births and fetal deaths  of 
22 or more completed weeks age of gestation  multiplied by 1,000 
   
Other mortality -related data covering other age groups and due to other 
causes/diseases of interest and relevance to the local health units, DOH and other 
concerned offices can be generated using the FHSIS Mortality database.  
 
Natality is another term for birth. Natality is usually expressed as a rate with the 
number of births in a given population over a given period of time (by dividing the total 
number of births by the total population multiplied by 1,000). The FHSIS records and 
reports the following natality data: 
 
 Livebirths. The total number of live births in a given year 
 Adolescent Birth Rate. The number of births to adolescent women aged 10-14 and 15-
19 years old per 1,000 women in the age group 
 Crude Birth Rate. The ratio of the total number of livebirths in a given population during 
a year to the mid-year population during a given period expressed per 1,000 population.

E.2  Using the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT) at the Municipal/City 
Level 
 
The supervising nurse/FHSIS Coordinator in the RHU/MHC/CHO records all the 
mortality and natality data into the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT). This becomes 
the source document of the nurse in coming up with the quarterly report on Mortality 
and Natality at the municipal/city level. 
 
1. The first column lists all the Mortality and Natality indicators being tracked by 
the health facility; 
 
2. The rest of the columns are intended for each of the barangays covered by 
the municipality/city. Write the name of each barangay per column; 
 
3. On a monthly basis, transfer the data pertinent to each Mortality and Natality 
indicator by barangay using the Monthly Report submitted by the Barangays  
as the source;  
 
4. Use this to prepare the quarterly report to be submitted to the next 
administrative level.

F. Reporting Service Coverage 
 
F.1 Monthly Mortality and Natality Report (M1) 
 
The Monthly Report is prepared by the midwife at the barangay level. The midwife fills 
up the Form to report the Mortality and Natality data from the first day to the last day 
of the month. This is submitted to the nurse at the RHU/MHC for consolidation. 
 
How to Accomplish the Monthly Report on Mortality and Natality 
 
1. The M1 Report is structured into 2 sections: 
 Section H.1. Mortality 
 Section H.2. Natality 
 
2. Column 1 lists the Mortality and Natality indicators to be reported. 
 
3. For Mortality Indicators, disaggregate the data into male and female while for 
Natality Indicator, d isaggregate the number of livebirths by age group of the 
mother (10-14, 15-19 and 20-49 years old). 
 
4. Write the sum of the counts for each indicator under the Total column. 
 
5. Compute for the rate of each indicator and write the result on the Rate column. 
 
 
 
F.2  Quarterly Report on Mortality and Natality (Q1) 
 
The Quarterly Report on Mortality and Natality  is the official health report of the 
municipality/city for the quarter.  It contains the consolidated three-month reports from 
all the BHS and RHU/MHC during the quarter . The PHN forwards this report to the 
Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO for prov incial consolidation. The 
municipality/city prepares only one quarterly report.  In case there is more than one 
RHU/MHC in the municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing 
the consolidation of all the quarterly data from different RHUs /MHCs and the 
preparation of one Quarterly Report for the municipality/city.  
 
How to Accomplish the Quarterly Report on Mortality and Natality 
 
1. The Q1 Report is structured into 2 sections: 
 Section H.1. Mortality 
 Section H.2. Natality
