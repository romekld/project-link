A.  Health Sector Performance Monitoring and Evaluation System 
 
The performance of the health sector is being tracked and measured through different 
monitoring and evaluation systems which the DOH, LGUs and the other partners had 
already put in place. As shown in the following figure, the health sector performance 
of the Philippines is categorized into long -term (Ambisyon Natin 2040), medium term 
(e.g. Philippine Development Plan, Philippine Health Agenda/National Objectives for 
Health which cover a 6 -year term), and in short -term (annual, semi-annual, quarterly, 
monthly) period. These short -term health performances are measured through the 
different management information systems such as the DOH Score Card, LGU Score 
Card and the Attached Agency Score Card.  
 
In particular, the FHSIS as a management information tool gen erates data from the 
local health facilities, consolidates and transmits them to the next administrative level 
until these reach the DOH -Central Office as one of its major sources for decision -
making. FHSIS data consolidated at each level of operation are also expected to be 
used by the LGUs in making decisions at their respective levels. The FHSIS is a 
source of selected indicators in scoring LGU performance, attached agencies’ 
performance as well as DOH performance both at the national and regional levels.

B.  Objectives of the FHSIS 
 
The FHSIS , as originally designed, is intended to record and track on a monthly, 
quarterly and annual basis the extent of coverage of essential public health services 
offered by the field health care facilities. The overall purpose of which is to guide the 
local health officers and coordinators in managing service delivery in their respective 
locality and to guide the DOH in crafting new policies/guidelines, prioritization and 
allocation of resources and improving quality of health care and services. While the 
overall objective of the FHSIS remains the same, it has begun to metamorphose over 
the last 10 years given the maturation of several public health programs, development 
and introduction of new health interventions, and adherence to global and national 
health sector principles and goals. Specifically, the FHSIS aims to:   
 
(1) provide timely update on the reach or coverage of public health services 
delivered through the network of public health facilities (e.g. BHS/health 
centers and RHUs/Main Health Centers) includi ng OPDs of government 
hospitals and private health facilities for certain health services;  
 
(2) make public health service coverage data available by socio -economic 
status (e.g. NHTS, IPs, etc.), vulnerable groups (pregnant, adolescent, 
infants, senior citizens) and source of service (private or public); 
 
(3) record and generate informat ion on monthly morbidity resulting from 
various causes, and quarterly mortality  rates and identifying the top ten 
leading  causes of diseases (all ages, and special age groups);   
 
(4) track, record and report information on demographic , nutritional status of 
0-59 months old children,  and selected infectious diseases data  on a 
yearly basis including school-based services. 
 
(5) develop and disseminate annual report on national, regional and local 
public health service coverage.

D. Forms and Tools 
 
The effectiveness and efficiency of the FHSIS as a management information system 
relies heavily on the completeness and accuracy of data being recorded as the clients 
consult and seek health services from the health facility. Prior to availing the services 
however, it is essential that the targeted recipients of health services are identified, 
located and registered. For this purpose, there is a need to profile each household 
within the catchment population of the health facili ty. The completed HH profiles 
become the basis of the health worker to construct the Master Lists of the different 
groups (e.g. pregnant, post -partum, children, adolescent, WRA, etc.) targeted to be 
given the appropriate services.

D.1 Household Profiles 
 
Profiling of households in a given barangay is the first step to ensure that all 
household members are identified and properly categorized by age group or health 
condition group. This 2018 FHSIS Version adopts the Community Health Team (CHT) 
Household Profiling Tool. This tool enables the volunteer health workers (e.g. BHWs, 
BNSs, etc.) to list all members of each household assigned to them and categorize 
them by sex and age and obtain other pertinent information (e.g. physical condition, 
NHTS members, etc.).  
 
Once these HH profiles are completed by the volunteer workers, the y are to submit 
these to the ir supervising midwives in the barangay . The Barangay midwife with 
assistance from the NDPs shall construct/update the Master List of each targeted  
group of clients to be served. It is advisable that the HH profiling will be done on 
January every year and to be updated during the first month of the subsequent 
quarters.  
 
D.2 Household Profiles and Master Lists  
 
As specified in Table 1. Scope of Services Covered by the FHSIS, several Master 
Lists must be prepared to ensure that all the targeted clients are identified and 
provided with the health care they need. It is important that only one Master List is 
generated for each target age group or health condition group. For example, the 
Master List of Pregnant Women to be provided with ANC check -ups should be the 
same list of pregnant women to be provided with oral health, deworming services and 
STI screening test and  treatment. The same is true for children targeted for 
micronutrient supplementation, immunization or deworming. Multiple Master Listing 
must be avoided to save time and resources and ensure consistency of data.   
 
In this regard, the Master Lists of Targe ted Clients must be constructed in an 
integrated manner based on the completed/updated HH profiles. The Master Lists 
then can be shared to all concerned health program providers who share the same 
target groups. It must be made clear at this point that the  FHSIS Target Client Lists 
(TCLs) was intended for the same purpose as the Master Lists. The difference 
however lies in the fact that most TCLs are filled up only when the clients seek 
consult the health provider. There is no proactive listing of all those  to be covered 
whether they have already availed or not the services.

2. Target Client List (TCL) 
 
The Target Client List is another tool which records the individual clients who must 
receive the essential package of services intended for their particular physiological 
condition. The TCL does not only list those individuals who have obtained  
services from the health facility but al l those targeted to receive said services. 
Hence, the TCLs  should contain all the names of individuals obtained through the 
completed HH Profiles from the volunteer workers.  The first part of the TCL is 
accomplished with the names of the targeted individuals and basic information such 
as address, age, birthday, sex. The other portion of the TCL is accomplished once the 
targeted client has already availed of services from the health care provider/facility. 
 
The TCLs allow the service providers to track and monitor the services provided to 
each individual client. Specifically, the tool enables the service provider to: 
 
 identify those who have been profiled but have not yet availed of services and 
therefore would requir e follow -up visit  by the volunteer workers or by the 
midwives/ nurses themselves;

D.5 Consolidation Tools 
 
There are basically two (2) forms designed to consolidate data from various reporting 
units/sources. These are Summary Tables (ST) and the Monthly Consolidation Tables 
(MCT).  
 
1. Summary Tables 
The Summary Table (ST) is a tool for the midwives to use in recording service 
coverage on a monthly basis for easier consolidation. It is usually designed into a 12 -
month column format representing each month of the year and the last column for the 
annual total.   
There are two Summary Tables designed for use by the midwives.

---

(1) Summary Table on Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage.  This 
summarizes all the data/indicators pertinent to program accomplishment. The 
first column lists all the indicators being tracked/measured with the 
accompanying “Target” in the Second Column. The subsequent columns 
represent each month of the year with quarterly breakdown and the annual 
total in the Last Column. If the indicator requires disaggregation by sex, age, 
socio-economic status (NHTS and non-NHTS), these are provided for on each 
monthly, quarterly annual basis.   
 
(2) Summary Table on Mortality and Natality. The midwife accomplishes this table 
on a monthly basis. The first column lists all the indicators being 
tracked/measured. The subsequent columns represent each month of the year 
with quarterly breakdown and the annual total in the Last Column. If the 
indicator requires disaggregation by sex or age group, these are provided for 
on each monthly, quarterly annual basis.   
 
2. Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT) 
 
This tool is to be used by the supervising nurse at the Municipal/City Health Office to 
record all data from all the barangays. This becomes the basis of the supervising 
nurse as reference in preparing the Quarterly Report. In short, the MCT is the output 
table of the M/CHO as it already contains the list of indicators by barangay. 
 
 
D.6 Reporting Forms and Tools 
 
The FHSIS Reporting Tools refer to those forms bearing the summary data that are to

1.1 Monthly Program Accomplishment or Service Coverage Report (M1) 
 
This Report contains the summary data of each indicator on program accomplishment

regard, the midwife should copy the data from the ST on  Program

health nurse / FHSIS coordinator at the MM/CHO level.  This tool  helps the midwife 
capture the monthly data so that it would be easier for the supervising nurse/FHSIS 
Coordinator to consolidate and prepare the Quarterly report.

2. Quarterly Report  
 
2.1 Quarterly Program Accomplishment Report /Service Coverage report (Q1) 
 
The Quarterly Form or the Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage Report is the 
official health report of the municipality/city for the quarter.  It contains the 
consolidated three month reports of all the BHSs and the RHU/MHC on program 
accomplishment/ service coverage categorized into: family health services, infectious 
disease prevention and control services, environmental health, and non -
communicable disease and prevention and control services during the quarter. It also 
contains report of all deaths which occurred in the municipality/city disaggregated by 
age and sex. Aside from program accomplishments and deaths, the quarterly reports 
also contains natality data for the quarter.  The PHN forwards this Q1 report to the 
Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO every Wednesday of the 2nd week of the 
1st month of the succeeding quarter for provincial consolidation. The municipality/city 
prepared only one quarterly report. In case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the 
municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolidation of 
all the quarterly data from different RHUs/MHCs and the preparation of one Quarterly 
Form for the municipality/city. Spaces are left blank for those indicators the 
municipality/city wants to generate based on their local needs and interests. 
   
3. Annual Report (A1) 
 
A1 is a reporting tool which contains data on nutrition (0 -59 months children who are 
stunted, wasted and obese), deworming, malaria and filariasis and demographic data

E. Flow of FHSIS Reports 
 
There are basically 4 categories of data/information that are to be collected and 
reported through the FHSIS. The flow of FHSIS Report is illustrated according to the 
category of data/information contained in each report.  
 
E.1 Flow of Report on Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage 
The flow of FHSIS Report on program accomplishment or service coverage begins 
with the report submitted by midwives in the BHS/HCs and RHUs/MHCs, OPD -
government and private hospitals and private health facilities where the services are 
provided. These are then consolidated at the M/CHO level and submitted to the next 
level of administration for provincial/city consolidation until these reach the DOH-CHD 
and Central Office.  
 
E.2 Flow of Report on Morbidity  
Note that data on morbidity diseases follows the same flow as that of the Program 
Accomplishment or Service Coverage Report 
 
E.3 Flow of Report on Demographic, Environmental, and Mortality 
The flow of FHSIS data on demographic profile, environmental health and sanitation 
natality and mortality vital health statistics reflect the Local Civil Registry Offices as 
another data source on top of the health facilities (BHS, HCs, RHUs, MHCs, OPD -
Hospitals, private clinics). The data flow shows the need for each local health facility  
to integrate those information from the LCRs before the report is submitted to the next 
level of administration. 
 
The following chart illustrates the overall flow of the FHSIS Reports from the time the 
client consults the facility until the report reaches  the DOH -Central Office.  As an 
overall policy, the reports of highly urbanized cities (HUCs) and independent cities 
(ICs) are submitted direct to the DOH-Center for Health Development.

F. Roles and Responsibilities 
 
The successful implementation of the FHSIS Version 2018 largely depends on the  
commitment and involvement of the following offices of the DOH and the LGUs at 
various levels of administration. It is therefore important to outline and define the roles 
and functions of every group of stakeholders concerned in the management and 
implementation of this nationwide information system.  
 
F.1 At the National Level: 
 
National Program Managers 
1. Establish and disseminate policies, guidelines and standards relative to the 
implementation of their respective programs;  
2. Define, set and update as needed the key indicators to be tracked relative to 
the implementation of their respective programs;  
3. Provide technical updates on their respective programs that may have bearing 
on the indicators being tracked/measured; 
4. Promote common understanding of the key indicators especially the definition, 
formula, data sources and frequency of measurements to be tracked and 
measured through FHSIS to their regional and local counterparts; 
5. Ensure that the same definition, formula, data sourc e and frequency of 
measurements are applied to any data management information system 
collecting the same indicators;  
6. Recommend mechanism/s to validate data collected from the field; 
7. Assist National FHSIS Coordinators review consolidated data on program 
indicators which may need to be further clarified with lower administrative 
levels;  
8. Review, analyze and utilize data for decision -making, policy formulation, 
budget and resource allocation/prioritization as well as enhancement of 
program implementation and service delivery; 
9. Participate in the data quality assurance activities. 
10. Disseminate results of the annual FHSIS Report pertinent to their respective 
programs. 
 
National FHSIS Coordinators 
1. Develop policies/standards/guidelines relative to the design and 
implementation of the FHSIS and ensure its adherence to the current DOH 
thrust and mandates; 
2. Orient/Train DOH management and technical staff on these policies/guidelines 
(e.g. FHSIS MOP) and ensure common understanding among those 
concerned; 
3. Cascade the same set of policies and guidelines to regional counterparts;  
4. Ensure the quality and timeliness of data submitted by the Regional FHSIS 
Coordinators;  
5. Consolidate, package and disseminate Regional FHSIS reports and produce 
the National FHSIS Report on an annual basis;
