A. Scope of NCD Services 
 
This is the first time that the accomplishment of the No n-Communicable Disease 
(NCD) Prevention and Control  Program will be tracked through the FHSIS. As such, 
only a few selected indicators are included given the level of maturity of the different 
NCD programs and how well the service delivery is in place at the different local 
health facilities nationwide. 
 
Lifestyle Related Diseases (LRDs) 
 Risk assessment on tobacco use, harmful use of alcohol, unhealthy diet  
 
Cancer Prevention and Control 
 Screening for cervical cancer 
 Screening for breast mass 
 Referral of clients with positive/suspect results 
 
Cardiovascular Disease Prevention and Control 
 Screening for hypertension 
 
Diabetes Mellitus Prevention and Control  
 Screening for diabetes mellitus 
  
Blindness Prevention Program 
 Screening for visual acuity and eye disease 
 Referral to appropriate eye care professional 
 
Immunization for Senior Citizens 
 Pneumococcal Polysaccharide Vaccine (PPV) Immunization for senior citizens 
60 years old and above

C. Master Listing of Adults and Senior Citizens 
 
Master Listing of adults and senior citizens is an integral part in the overall delivery of 
adult and senior citizen care and services. This will serve as the principal reference of 
the health care providers  to identify and  stratify clients for registration into different 
health programs appropriate for their age group . This will also aid them in prioritizing 
those to be visited and followed up and to be provided with information and services.   
Proactively identifying and master listing of adults  and senior citizens  will increase 
service coverage and provide the entire population to be reached out and served. 
This master list is to be sourced from the completed and updated Household Profiles 
(Chapter 3). Following are the steps in master listing the adults and senior citizens. 
 
1. Based on the completed HH Profiles in each barangay (Chapter 3), the BHS  
midwife with assistance from BHWs/NDPs other volunteer workers shall 
summarize all those profiled belonging to the following age groups: 
 Part 1. List of Adults 20 to 59 years old 
 Part 2. List of Senior Citizens 60 years old and above  
 
2. Transfer the following information from the accomplished HH profiles into the 
Master List of Adults 20 to 59 years old and Senior Citizens: 
- HH number/Family Serial Number  
- Name of Adult 20-59 years old and Senior Citizens 
- Address of Adult 20-59 years old and Senior Citizens 
- Sex 
- Age and Birthday of each Adult 20-59 years old and Senior Citizen 
- Socio-Economic Status: NHTS member or Non-NHTS 
 
3. The existing Registries (Blue/Red Book) for NCD could serve as the initial Master 
List of adults 2 0-59 years old and senior citizens . Validate if the names of the 
adults and senior citizens from the accomplished HH Profiles are already in the 
NCD Registries/Blue & Red Book. If not, include the names on the list. 
 
4. The BHS midw ife shall file and maintain these  Master Lists. These M aster Lists 
will be useful as reference for  immunization and visual acuity screening of senior 
citizens, Cervical and Breast Cancer Screening for 20 years old and above female 
adults and CVD/NCD Risk Assessment among 20 years old and above adults.

D. Provision of Preventive, Diagnosis and Management Services  
 
D.1 Individual Treatment Record 
 
As clients consult and  avail of service s from the health facility, they are initially 
registered on the Individual Treatment Records (ITR) used by the health facility in 
general with the following basic information:  
 Demographic Profile: Complete Nam e, Address, Age, Birthday, Sex,  
Occupation 
 Vital Signs:  BP, RR, HR, Temp, etc. 
 Anthropometric Measurements: weight, height, BMI 
 Chief Complaint  
 Diagnosis, management and treatment 
 
Clients 20 years old and above seeking consultation at the health facility must be risk-
assessed using the PhilPEN  protocol, including breast examination, cervical cancer 
screening. As a guide, the service provider shall: 
 
1. Administer the Philippine P ackage of Essential NCD (PhilPEN)  Interventions 
(CVD/NCD Risk Assessment Form)  for the lifestyle -related diseases (LRDs) : 
tobacco use, harmful use of alcohol, unhealthy diet, hypertension and 
diabetes mellitus. 
 
2. Administer the Client Assessment, Screening, Diagnosis and Treatment for 
Cervical Cancer (CASDT) and Breast Cancer Form for clients seeking 
screening for breast and cervical cancer. 
 
3. For eye disease screening, use the Eye and Vision Assessment Form. 
 
4. Ensure that all items in the ITRs are filled -up upon interview or screening of 
the client; 
 
5. At the end of the day, collate all the accomplished ITRs as basis in 
accomplishing and/or updating the NCD Registries. 
 
6. Maintain the accomplished ITRs and use the same to record the results of the 
follow-up visits of the client to the health facility.

to facilitate the capture and recall of data.  These are health facility-based documents

performance using these data.   
 
The first column lists exhaustively the indicators of the NCD Program in the barangay.  
In some Summary Tables, there is a “Target” column where you will place, at the start 
of the year, the targets of your barangay for each Indicator. It is advisable that these 
be discussed with the nurse superv isor. Under each succeeding monthly columns, 
record the number being asked that corresponds to each indicator for the month. 
Under each quarter, write the totals required.

Level

succeeding column, write the name of each BHS on top and the corresponding

F. Reporting on a Monthly and Quarterly Basis 
 
F.1 Monthly Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage Report (M1) 
 
The Monthly NCD Program Accomplishment Report (M1) is prepared by the midwife 
at the barangay level. The midwife fills up the form to report the program 
accomplishments from the first day to the last day of the month. She submits this to 
the nurse at the RHU/MHC for consolidation. 
 
Note that the  Monthly Program Accomplishment Re port contains the 
accomplishments of all public health programs being tracked through the FHSIS. The 
form is divided into sections where the various indicators per program are listed.   
 
F.2 Quarterly Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage Report (Q1) 
The Quarterly NCD Program Accomplishment Report (Q1) is the official health report 
of the municipality/city for the quarter. It contains the consolidated three month reports 
from all the BHS and RHU/MHC during the quarter. The PHN forwards this report to 
the Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO every third week of the first month of the 
succeeding quarter  for provincial conso lidation. The municipality/city prepares only 
one quarterly report.  In case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the 
municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolidation of 
all the quarterly data from different RHUs/MHCs and the  preparation of one Quarterly 
Report for the municipality/city.  
 
The Quarterly NCD Report includes NCD prevention and control service indicators for 
the quarter with columns on eligible population and with segregation by sex and 
socio-economic status: whether the client is a member of the NHTS Household or not. 
It also provides columns for interpretation and recommendations.
