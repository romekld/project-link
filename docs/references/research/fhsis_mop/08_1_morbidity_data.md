A. Disease Definitions/Classifications 
 
Morbidity is another term for illness. A person can have simultaneous illnesses or co-
morbidities at the same time. The FHSIS records and reports all the diseases that 
occurred and seen in the health facilities throughout the year. These diseases 
recorded and reported through the FHSIS follow the same definitions provided for by 
the Philippine Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response (PIDSR) . 
Corresponding ICD-10 Codes are also provided to ensure harmonized classifications 
of diseases across all recording and reporting units.

C. Recording and Reporting Morbidities 
 
At the BHS level, the diseases/morbidities are entered into the morbidity logbook or 
registry and consolidated by the midwife at the end of the month using the FHSIS 
Monthly Morbidity Disease Report Form for the Barangay (M2  Section A.1).  This is 
then submitted to the RHU /MHC.  At the RHU/MHC, the PHN consolidates all the 
submitted M2s and generates the RHU /MHC Report using the M2 for the RHU/MHC. 
This is submitted to the PHO /CHO.  The M2 forms are also used by the PHO /CHOs 
to submit reports to DOH-CHDs then the DOH-CHDs use the same forms to submit to 
the DOH-CO. The nurse/midwife at the health facility summarizes the diseases in the 
Monthly Report on Ten Leading Causes of Morbidity ( M2 Section A .2) by specific 
age/vulnerable group or sex and will be submitted to the next administrative level. 
 
 A client will be counted as a case if there is an occurrence of a 
disease/condition diagnosed by the physician. 
 If the client was cured from his disease/condition after the medical intervention 
was given and returns to the health facility due to re -occurrence of the same 
condition, the client will be counted as another case. This means that it is 
possible for one client to be rep orted of having several cases of the same 
disease in a year. 
 A client who has co-morbidities will be counted as a case for each disease. 
 The client’s succeeding visits to the health facility for re-supply of maintenance 
medications will not be counted as a  case unless the client develops a new 
disease. 
 
C.1 Monthly Report of Morbidity/Diseases (M2 Section A1) 
 
The Monthly Report of Morbidity Diseases (M2 Section A1) contains a list of all 
diseases by age group and sex. It summarizes the monthly report of 
morbidities/diseases.  
 
1. Write the full name of the BHS/BHC, RHU/MHC, the month and the year for 
which the report is being prepared.   
 
2. The first column lists the specific diseases with corresponding ICD-10 Codes 
in the next column.  
 
3. After this list, another set of disease are specified but without ICD-10 Codes. 
Refer to their definitions as provided above.  
 
4. Based on the Disease/Morbidity Registry, count the number of clients seen in 
the health facility with the specific illnesses/diseases for the month. 
Disaggregate the counts by specific age groups and by sex.

C.2 Monthly Report on Ten Leading Causes of Morbidity (M2 Section A2) 
 
The Monthly Report of Morbidity Diseases (M2 Section A2) contains a list of all the 
Ten Leading Causes of Morbidity by specific age/vulnerable group or sex. It 
summarizes the monthly report of morbidities/diseases.  
 
1. Write the full name of the health facility, barangay/municipality/city/province, 
the projected population for the year and the year for which the report is being 
prepared.   
 
2. The first column lists the ten (10) leading causes of morbidity for a specific 
age/vulnerable group or sex arranged according to the highest number of 
cases and rate. 
 
3. Indicate in the second column the number of cases for each disease for a 
specific age/vulnerable group or sex and the corresponding rate per 100,000 
population in the third column.
