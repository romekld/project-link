Chapter 8.3 
Recording and Reporting Demographic Data 
 
A. Demographic Data 
 
The FHSIS  also tracks and monitors community -based or population -based data 
which are only reported on an annual basis. These refer particularly to demographic 
data in a given municipality/city. T his chapter summarizes the indicators to be 
collected and reported including the FHSIS forms to be used. 
 
The FHSIS reports the following demographic data on an annual basis. 
 
 Population 
 No. of Barangays  
 No. of Households 
 No. of Health Centers: Municipal Health Centers, City Health Centers and 
Rural Health Units 
 No. of Barangay Health Stations 
 No. of local health personnel by category: physicians/doctors, dentists, 
nurses, midwives, medical technologists, nutritionists/dietitians, sanitary 
engineers, sanitary inspectors and active barangay health workers 
 Health personnel by category to Population Ratio

C. Consolidating Demographic Data in the Health Facility 
 
C.1 Using the Annual Consolidation Table (ACT) on Demographic Data 
 
All Demographic Data is recorded into the Annual Consolidation Table ( ACT). This 
becomes the source document of the midwife/nurse in coming up with the annual 
report on Demographic Data of the health facility. 
 
1. The first column lists all the Demographic Data indicators being tracked by 
the health facility; 
 
2. The rest of the columns are intended for each of the health facility covered 
by the reporting unit. Write the name of each health facility per column; 
 
3. Provide the overall total for the year at the last column. 
 
4. Use this to prepare the annual report to be submitted to the next 
administrative level.

D. Recording and Reporting Demographic Data Using the Annual 
Report Form (A1) 
 
The Demographic Data are recorded and reported through the Annual Report Form  
(A1). This form contains basic information about the 
barangay/municipality/city/province/region in terms of its total catchment population , 
households, barangays , the number of existing health facilities including HCs and 
BHS in the loca lity as well as the number of health personnel hired by the LGU  or 
through the Human Resource for Health (HRH) Deployment Program. These data are 
submitted once year. 
 
Population Write on the space provided the total population 
according to PSA latest census or the Projected 
Population computed by EB. 
No. of Barangays Write on the space provided the actual number of 
barangays of the municipality/city. 
No. of Households Write on the space provide the total number of 
households of the municipality/city according to 
PSA latest census or the Projected Number of 
Households computed by EB. 
No. of Health Centers Write on the space provided the number of existing 
main health centers in the municipality/city. 
No. of BHS Write on the space provided the actual number of 
barangay health stations. A BHS can be 
considered a reporting unit if the following 
conditions are satisfied: 
a. It renders/delivers health services to a defined 
catchment area which may be composed of 
one or more barangays. 
b. A midwife renders regular service to the area.  
In case where the midwife of the area is in 
prolonged leave of absence or resigned but a 
replacement is expected, the BHS remains a 
reporting unit. The reports are expected to be 
submitted by the nurse or midwife(s) who took 
over the servicing of the area. 
c. Health services may be provided from any 
physical structure designated for the purpose 
i.e. a BHS building, a barangay hall or a place 
of residence. 
d. The catchment area served is not a service 
area of any RHU.  For instance, Poblacion in 
most cases is the catchment area served by 
the RHU. Thus, the Poblacion BHS cannot be

The Annual Report on Demographic Data is the official health report of the 
barangay/municipality/city for the year. The midwife fills up the form to report the 
Demographic Data of the year . This is submitted to the nurse at the RHU/MHC for 
consolidation. The PHN forwards this report to the Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at 
the PHO for provincial consolidation. The municipality/city prepares only one annual 
report. I n case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the municipality/city, the 
MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolidation of all the annual data 
from different RHUs/MHCs an d the preparation of one Annual Report for the 
municipality/city.  
 
How to Accomplish the Annual Report on Demographic Data 
 
1. Column 1 lists the indicators to be reported 
 
2. For the number of health workers, d isaggregate the data into LGU-hired and 
DOH-hired. 
 
3. Write the total or sum of the counts for each indicator under the Total column. 
 
4. Compute for the Ratio to Population of each indicator and write the result on the 
Ratio to Population column. 
 
5. Analyze the data and write the interpretation and Recommendations/Actions to 
be Taken under the appropriate column. 
This Annual Report on Demographic Data shall be incorporated into 
the overall Annual Report (A1) to be submitted by the health facility 
to the higher administration together with the accomplishments of 
the other public health programs.
