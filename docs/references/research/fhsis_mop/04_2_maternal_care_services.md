Chapter 4.2  
Maternal Care and Services 
 
 
A. Scope of Maternal Care and Services 
 
Maternal Care encompasses the package of health interventions and services that 
must be provided to women from the time they become pregnant, during delivery and 
postpartum period which is up to 42 days after delivery. The following enumerates the 
key maternal care and services that are tracked through the FHSIS. 
 
Prenatal Care 
 At least 4 prenatal check-ups 
 Nutritional status by measurement of body mass index 
 Immunization against Tetanus diphtheria (2 doses for first pregnancy, at least 
3 doses for 2nd or more pregnancy) 
 Micronutrient supplem entation of iron with folic acid  (some LGUs have 
separate iron and folic acid), calcium carbonate, iodine capsule  
 One dose of deworming (anti-helminthic) tablet 
 STI Testing: Syphilis, Hepatitis B and HIV 
 Laboratory Testing: complete blood count or hemoglobin & hematocrit count, 
gestational diabetes 
 
Intrapartum Care and Delivery Outcome 
 Livebirths by birth weight,  
 Deliveries attended by skilled health professionals 
 Deliveries in health facilities 
 Deliveries by type: vaginal, cesarean section 
 Pregnancy outcome: full-term, pre-term, fetal death, abortion/miscarriage 
 
Postpartum Care 
 Given with at least 2 postpartum check-ups together with their newborns 
within 7 days after delivery 
 Micronutrient supplementation of iron with folic acid for 3 months 
 Provision of 1 dose of Vitamin A supplementation within 1 month after delivery 
 
 
B. Metadata 
 
The following table lists, defines and rationalizes the key indicators on Maternal Care 
to be tracked through the FHSIS . The metadata also provides the formula in 
computing the indicators, specify the data sources as well as the frequency of 
measurements.

C. Master Listing of Pregnant and Postpartum Women in the 
Pregnancy Tracking Form 
 
Master Listing of pregnant  and postpartum  women is an essential process in t he 
overall delivery of maternal care. It serves as the main reference of health providers in 
prioritizing those to be visited and followed up and to be provided with information and 
services. Proactively identifying pregnant women is expected to reduce lat e consults 
at the health facility, thus improving the proportion of those with at least 1 prenatal 
check-up during the first trimester, and thereby improving the quality of care received 
by the pregnant women. The following are the steps in master listing the pregnant and 
postpartum women.  
 
1. Based on the completed HH Profiles in each barangay (discussed in Chapter 
3), the BHS midwife with assistance from the BHWs/NDPs/other volunteer 
workers shall summarize all  those profiled who are pregnan t and those who 
have just given birth (0-42 days from giving birth); 
 
2. Transfer the following information from the accomplished HH Profiles into the 
Pregnancy Tracking Form; 
 Name of the pregnant women 
 Age 
 Gravidity 
 Parity 
 Expected Date of Delivery 
 Antenatal Care Check-Ups 
 Pregnancy Outcome 
 Mother and Child Postnatal Check-ups 
 Civil Registration 
 
3. Validate if the pregnant women or the postpartum women from the HH Profiles 
are already recorded in the Pregnancy Tracking Form and are already availing 
services from the health facility; 
 
4. If the pregnant or postpartum women are not yet registered in the Pregnancy 
Tracking Form, the Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) and other community 
volunteers including the NDPs with the overall supervision of the BHS 
midwives shall visit the pregnan t or postpartum women concerned and 
encourage them to visit and consult at the health facility. At this point, the 
BHW/Other volunteer workers/NDPs may already administer the Pregnancy 
Tracking Tool to the pregnant women; 
 
5. The BHS midwife shall file and ma intain the Pregnancy Tracking Form  for 
Maternal Care and Services; 
 
6. The BHW/other volunteers/NDPs shall maintain the accomplished Pregnancy 
Tracking Form to monitor the subsequent check-ups/consultations of the 
pregnant women at the health facility.

D.  Provision of Prenatal and Postpartum Care Using the 
Maternal Client Record for Prenatal, Postpartum and 
Newborn Care 
 
As the pregnant/postpartum women seek consult and avail of services from the health 
facility, they are initially registered on the Individual Treatment Records (ITR) used by 
the health facility in general with the following basic information:  
 
 Demographic Profile: Complete Name, Address, Age, Birthday, Sex, Religion, 
Occupation 
 Vital Signs:  BP, RR, etc. 
 Anthropometric Measurements: weight, height, BMI 
 Chief Complaint  
 Diagnosis, management and treatment 
 
The pregnant /postpartum women are further assessed using the Maternal Client 
Record for Prenatal Care (MCRPC)  and the Maternal Client Record for Postpartum 
and Neonatal Care (MCRPNC) . The MCRPC Form records the demographic -socio-
economic profile of the client, their medical, family, past health, social, obstetrical and 
family planning history. It also records the results of physical examination undertaken. 
The back-page records the results, advice and services provided every consult of the 
pregnant women at the health facility including the results of the abdominal 
examination. 
 
On the other hand, the MCRPNC also records the demographic, socioeconomic 
profile of the postpartum women includ ing the results of physical examination and 
assessment. Results of the newborn assessment are also recorded in the MCRPNC 
Form. Just like the MCR for Prenatal Care , spaces are also provided to record the 
results every time the postpartum woman consults the health facility.  
 
 As a guide, the service provider shall: 
1. Administer the Maternal Cl ient Record for Prenatal Care after obtaining the 
basic information about the pregnant and postpartum women using the 
generic ITR of the health facility; 
2. Ensure that all  items in the MCRPC /MCRPNC Forms are filled -up upon 
interview or screening the client; 
3. For Pregnant Women, administer next the Birth and Emergency Plan; 
4. Collate all accomplished MCRPC/MCRPNC Forms and the Birth Emergency 
Plan at the end of each day as basis in accomplishing the Target Client List for 
Prenatal Care and the TCL for Postpartum and Neonatal Care; 
5. Maintain the accomplished  MCRPC Forms and use the same to record the 
results of the follow up check-ups of the client to the health facility.

F. Consolidating Prenatal, Intrapartum, and Postpartum-
Newborn Care Data by Barangay  
 
F.1 Using the Summary Table on Maternal Care and Services at the 
Barangay Level 
 
The Summary Table on Maternal Care and Services is intended to record prenatal , 
delivery outcome  and postpartum -newborn care services rendered by the health 
facility to facilitate the capture and recall of data.   
 
1. The first column  lists of the Prenatal Car e, Del ivery Outcome  and 
Postpartum - Newborn Care indicators to  be  tracked  by your health 
facility; 
 
2. Place under the second column the targets of your health facility for each 
indicator at the start of the year.  Consult the supervising nurse for the 
number to be entered under this column; 
 
3. Under each succeeding monthly columns , record the number 
corresponding to each indicator for the month;  
 
4. Under each quarter columns, write the total number of each target 
rendered with the services for the 3-month period. 
 
5. Provide the overall total of each indicator for the year at the last column. 
 
F.2 Using the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT)  
 
The supervising nurse/FHSIS Coordinator in the RHU/MHC/CHO records all 
prenatal care, delivery outcome and postpartum – newborn data from all barangays 
into the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT). This becomes the source document of 
the nurse in coming up with the quarterly report on Prenatal Care , Delivery Outcome 
and Postpartum-Newborn Care at the municipal/city level.  
 
1. The firs t column  lists all the Prenatal Care , Delivery Outcome  and 
Postpartum - Newborn Care indicators being tracked by your  health 
facility; 
 
2. The rest of the columns are intended for each of the barangays covered by 
the municipality/city. Write the name of each barangay per column; 
 
3. On a monthly basis, transfer the data pertinent to each Prenatal Care , 
Delivery Outcome, and Postpartum - Newborn Care indicator by barangay 
using the Monthly Report submitted by the Barangays as the source;  
 
4. Use this to prepare the monthly and quarterly report to be submitted to the 
next administrative level.

G. Reporting Maternal Service Coverage 
 
G.1 Monthly Maternal Care Report (M1) 
The Monthly Maternal Care Program Report contains  the indicators b eing tracked 
through the FHSIS  which are sourced from the accomplished Summary Table. This 
Report is divided into sub -sections as prenatal care, intrapartum care and delivery 
outcomes, and postpartum and newborn care.  
 
How to Accomplish the Monthly Report on Maternal Care 
1. Provide the total projected population of the year on the first row. 
2. Column 1 lists the Maternal Care and Services indicators to be reported to the 
next higher level of administration. 
3. Based on the accomplished Summary Table, indicate the number pertinent to 
each indicator as listed in Column 1. 
4. Disaggregate the number according to the age of the pregnant or post partum 
women under Column 2. Indicate these under the appropriate sub -columns 
provided for 10-14, 15-19 and 20-49 years old. Provide totals under column 3 
and the Remarks in Column 4. 
 
G.2 Quarterly Program Accomplishment Report on Maternal Care (Q1) 
The Quarterly Form is the official health report of the municipality/city for the quarter.  
It contains the consolidated three -month reports from all  the BHSs and RHU s/MHCs 
during the quarter. The PHN forwards this report to the Provincial /City FHSIS 
Coordinator at the PHO /City every third week of the first month of the succeeding 
quarter for provincial /city consolidation. The municipality/city prepare s only one 
quarterly report.  In case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the municipality/city, 
the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolidation of all the quarterly 
data from different RHUs/MHCs and the preparation of one Quarterly Form for  the 
municipality/city.  
 
How to Accomplish the Quarterly Report on Maternal Services 
1. Column 1 lists the indicators on maternal services being tracked by the 
FHSIS. 
2. Determine the total number of eligible population pertinent to each indicator 
and write these under Column 2. 
3. Disaggregate the number of each indicator by the age of the pregnant or post-
partum women and write these on the appropriate sub-columns. 
4. Compute for the proportion/percent corresponding to each age-group with the 
number of eligible of population as denominator. 
5. Analyze the level of program accomplishment and write under Column 5 and 
the recommended actions under Column 6. 
Note that the M1 and Q1 Reports on Maternal Care (Section B) 
will be submitted by the Health Facility FHSIS Staff together 
with the other M1 and Q1 Reports on other Public Health 
Programs

I. Additional Guides in Recording and Reporting Maternal 
Services 
 
Maternal health care and services provided by the private health facilities can be 
included in the numerator as long as there is an established partnership between the 
DOH/LGU and the private health facilities. This assum es that the private health 
facilities use the same definitions and formulas of the indicator used by the public 
health facilities. 
 
I.1 Proportion of Pregnant Women with At least 4 ANC Check-ups 
 
1. If a pregnant woman received all her prenatal check -ups in a  private facility 
following the standard definition of 4ANC set by the DOH  upon validation by 
the local health staff  based on the mother’s record and/or check-up at the 
private health facility, this can be included in the count. 
 
2. In high-end areas (e.g. private subdivisions, etc.) where most pregnant women 
had prenatal check-ups with their private physicians, they cannot be included 
in the report. However, if the public health facility conducted home or follow-up 
check-up to th e pregnant women  with proof of  prenatal check -up from the 
private facility (presence of mother and child book/card) then the pregnant 
woman can be reported in the 4ANC.  
 
I.2 Intrapartum (Classifying deliveries) 
 
1. Birth in an ambulance is considered facility-based delivery provided that this is 
DOH-licensed and complies with the national policy on ambulance use and 
services (AO 2010-0003 & AO 2016-0029) 
 
2. Birth in an emergency transport is not considered a facility based delivery 
unless the completion of the birthing process happened in t he health facility 
(expulsion of the placenta).  
 
3. If delivery happens in transit with the full expulsion of the placenta, it is 
considered as non-health facility based delivery. 
 
4. Regardless of the number of children delivered (in the case of multiple birth s), 
the number of deliveries should only be counted as one. This should be 
counted where the placenta was completely expelled. 
 
I.3 Proportion of Postpartum Women with At least 2 PP Check-ups 
 
1. Consider a woman who delivered in a health facility and discharged only after 
24 hours to have received the first postpartum check-up. 
 
2. Postpartum check-up can be provided in a health facility or at home. 
 
I.4 Proportion of women less than 10 years old and more than 50 years old 
provided with Maternal Care and Services 
 
1. Record any woman less than 10 years old  and more than 50 years old  
provided with Maternal Care (Prenatal, Intrapartum, Postpartum) in the TCL
