Chapter 9.4 
Monitoring the Implementation of the 2018 FHSIS 
 
In addition to accuracy of data (discussed in Chapter 9.1), the other 2 essential 
elements that need to be monitored in the process of implementing the 2018 FHSIS 
to ensure its usefulness and relevance are: (i) timeliness of report submission and (ii) 
completeness of data.  Mechanisms must be put in place to ensure the FHSIS’ 
compliance to these 2 elements. 
 
A.  Monitoring Timeliness of Report Submission  
 
Delayed submission of reports was surfaced as one of the major challenges in FHSIS 
operations during c onsultations among regional and local FHSIS Coordinators  and 
program managers. Late or delayed submission from one reporting unit or 2 has great 
bearing on the quality of data being used for decision making. Prompt monitoring  
therefore of the status of report submission from lower levels is vital in the overall 
effectiveness  and efficiency of the system.  
 
1. Timeliness of submission is anchored on the agreed -upon due dates of 
submission for different FHSIS reports ( M1, M2, Q1, A1 ) at var ious levels of 
operations. These are specified in Chapter 2, Table 2. 
 
2. Consolidation at a higher level will be done immediately after submission.  
Changes will be incorporated on the next reporting period. 
 
3. Though the FHSIS standard requires all reports to be submitted on time, there are 
circumstances that prevent reporting units/lower levels to do so. In this regard, it 
is important for the FHSIS Coordinators undertaking the monitoring to specify the 
extent of submission every reporting period. 
 
4. The purpose  of monitoring the timeliness of report submission is to have an 
immediate follow-up of those reporting units not submitting on time and to provide 
them with technical assistance as needed. FHSIS Coordinators should be able to 
identify at once those report ing units which are not submitting reports on time, 
and provide them the technical assistance needed. 
 
B.  Monitoring Completeness of Data 
 
There are 2 levels in measuring the completeness of FHSIS data.  
 
Level 1. Completeness of Data by Reporting Units means that all the BHS/HC are 
collecting and submitting FHSIS data to the next level of health care.  Failure to do so 
will not provide a complete picture of the heath coverage of the particular 
area/locality. For this purpose, it is important that the FH SIS Coordinator o r Point 
Person at various levels of administration must be able to track the completeness of 
reporting units submitting reports to their respective levels.

Level 2. Completeness of Data by Cluster of Public H ealth Program/Cluster 
means that all the public health program care and services covered by the FHSIS are 
reported and available  except in those areas where the services are not applicable 
(e.g. prevention and control of leprosy, malaria, rabies, etc.) This requires that data as 
specified in the Meta data for on Family Health Care and Service, Infectious Disease 
Prevention and Control, Non-Communicable Disease and Prevention and Control and 
Environmental Health and Sanitation are collected, reported and analyzed.  These 
also include the morbidity, mortality and demographic data that must be made 
available on an annual basis. For this purpose, the same FHSIS Coordinator/Point 
Person must be able to track if complete data on program services are submitted by 
the reporting units. 
 
C. Guide and Tool in Monitoring Timeliness of Report Submission and 
Completeness of Data Submitted 
 
1. Monitoring the timeliness of report submission and completeness of data submitted 
by the reporting units must be done at the same time and must be recorded in one 
monitoring form. Attached is the recommended format for monitoring these 2 
elements and is applicable at all levels of operations. However, you must note the 
different information to be inputted for each specific level and for each type o f 
report (M1, Q1, A1) as these have different due dates. 
 
2. List the reporting units under your jurisdiction under the First Column. For 
example, the FHSIS Point Person at the municipal/city level must write the 
names of barangays under the First Column. For  the PHO/ CHO level, list the 
name of catchment munic ipalities/cities. For the DOH -CHD, you must list the 
names of provinces and HUCs in your region. At the DOH -EB level, write the 17 
DOH Regions.  
 
3. Take note that for each level of monitoring, there are different types of reports 
each level need to submit . For example, BHS/barangay level needs to submit 
monthly and annual FHSIS data to the municipal/city level while the municipal/city 
level will only be submitting quarterly and annual report to the PHO/HUC level. 
The same is true for the DOH-RO to DOH-EB. 
 
4. Specify the due dates each type of report must be submitted for each level.  
 
5. In monitoring timeliness of report submission: 
5.1 Place a check mark (√) under Column 2 if the reporting unit submitted the 
report and cross (X) if not; 
5.2 Write on Column 3 the actual date the report/s was received. 
5.3 Assess and classify the timeliness of submission: 
OT: ON Time , this means that the report was received before or on the 
agreed upon due date 
  
5.4 Count the number of reporting units by the timeliness status and indicate this 
at the bottom of the list. Compute the percentages of the total reporting units.

5.5 All reporting units not submitting on time (OT) must be followed up at once by 
the FHSIS point person and provide assistance as appropriate. 
 
6. In monitoring completeness of data by reporting units: 
 
6.1 Ba sed on the report /s submitted , check if the report/s  contain the public 
health programs/cluster expected to be submitted on the particular period. 
For example, demographic data are only due on annual basis. Do not expect 
to see these data if you are monitoring M1 and Q1 Reports. 
 
6.2 For each barangay/municipality/city/province/region, check if the reports 
contain data on the fo llowing clusters. Indicate the total number of public 
health programs/c luster data submitted over the total no. of program 
expected to be reported. For example, if the reporting unit/level submitted 
data on all Family Health Services, place 4/4. If newbor n care was not 
included, place 3/4. Do the same for the other cluster of services

assistance provided which could be useful for the next reporting period.
