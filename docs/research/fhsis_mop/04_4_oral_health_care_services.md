Chapter 4.4 
Oral Health Care and Services 
 
A. Scope of Oral Health Care and Services 
Oral Health services are offered by the local health facilities to clients at varying age 
groups. The FHSIS monitors and tracks the provision of these services at the 
RHU/MHC as dentists are deployed only at the municipal/city level . The following 
enumerates the key oral health services for each age group. 
 
0-11 months (infants) 
 Oral Examination 
 Instruction on infant’s oral health care 
 Advice on exclusive breastfeeding 
 Topical application of fluoride (at 9-11 mos.) (unless contraindicated) 
 
1- 4 years old (12-59 months old children)  
 Oral Examination 
 Topical application of fluoride  
 Supervised Tooth Brushing 
 Oral Health Education 
 Atraumatic Restorative Treatment (ART) 
 Oral prophylaxis/scaling 
 
5-9 years old (school-aged children) 
 Oral Examination 
 Supervised Tooth Brushing 
 Oral Health Education 
 Pit and fissure sealant application 
 Temporary filling 
 Permanent filling 
 
10-14 and 15-19 years old (adolescents) 
 Oral Examination 
 Education and counselling on good oral hygiene, diet and adverse effects 
of tobacco/smoking and alcohol and sweetened beverages & food 
 Pit and fissure sealant application 
 Temporary filling 
 Permanent filling 
 Oral prophylaxis/scaling       
 Oral Urgent Treatment (OUT) 
 
20- 59 years old (adults) 
 Oral Examination 
 Education and counseling on good oral hygiene, diet and adverse effects 
of tobacco/smoking and alcohol and sweetened beverages & food           
 Gum Treatment               
 Oral prophylaxis/scaling                      
 Permanent filling 
 Atraumatic Restorative Treatment (ART)

C. Master Listing of Oral Health Care Clients 
 
Master Listing of oral health care clients is an essential process in the overall delivery 
of oral health care . It serves as the main reference of health providers in prioritizing 
those to be visited and followed up and to be provided with information and services. 
Proactively identifying clients for oral health care  is expected to reduce late consults 
at the health fa cility, thus improving the proportion of those who are orally fit , and 
thereby improving the quality of care received by the clients. The following are the 
steps in master listing the oral health care clients.  
 
1. Based on the completed HH Profiles in each barangay (discussed in Chapter 
3), the BHS midwife with assistance from the BHWs/NDPs/other volunteer 
workers shall summarize all those profiled who are eligible for oral health care 
services; 
 
2. Transfer the following information from the accomplished HH Profiles into the 
Master List of Oral Health Care Clients; 
 Name of the client 
 Address 
 Sex 
 Age 
 Date of Birth 
 Socio-economic Status 
 
3. Validate if the client from the HH Profiles are already recorded in the Master 
List of Oral Health Care Clients  and are already availing services from the 
health facility; 
 
4. If the client is not yet registered in the Master List of Oral Health Care Clients, 
the Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) and other community volunteers 
including the NDPs with the overall supervision of the BHS midwives shall visit 
the client concerned and encourage him to visit and consult at the health 
facility. At this point, the BHW/Other volunteer workers/NDPs may already 
administer the master list to the client; 
 
5. The BHS midwife shall submit the Master List of Oral Health Care Clients to 
the RHU nurse. 
 
6. The RHU nurse/staff  shall maintain the accomplished Master List of Oral 
Health Care Clients to monitor the subsequent check -ups/consultations of the 
clients at the health facility.

D.  Provision of Oral Health Care Using Individual Treatment 
Record (ITR) 
 
The provision of oral health care and services is recorded on the Individual Treatment 
Record using the standard format, the health facility’s ITR must be able to capture the 
necessary information needed about the client (demographic, vital signs, dental 
history, etc.), able to record the results of consultations or examinations undertaken 
as well as t he specific services provided.  All ITRs must be properly filed and 
maintained for five (5) years by the Oral Health Clinic staff to facilitate retriev al of 
records for those requiring follow-up visits or check-ups. 
 
Guide: 
 
1. Ensure that all items in the ITRs are filled -up upon interview or screening of 
the client; 
 
2. At the end of the day, collate all the accomplished ITRs as basis in 
accomplishing the Target Client List for Oral Health Care; 
 
3. Maintain the accomplished ITRs and use the same to record the results of the 
follow-up visits of the client to the health facility.

E. Recording the Services Given to Children into the Target Client 
List 
 
At the end of each day, it is important for the Municipal/City Oral Health Clinic staff to 
transfer from the ITR the information that need to be reported to the next 
administrative level into the Target Client List for Oral Health Care and Services . 
These data are to be reported through the FHSIS. This form must be properly filled-up 
and updated as soon as possible following the client’s visit.  
 
How to Accomplish the Oral Health Care Target Client List 
 
The Oral Health Care Target Client List  shall be prepared by the end o f each day 
based on accomplished ITRs. This is designed according to age group as follows: 
 Infants: 0-11 months old 
 Pre-school: 1-4 years old (12-59 months old) 
 School-age: 5-9 years old 
 Adolescents: 10-14 and 15-19 years old 
 Adults: 20-59 years old 
 Senior Citizens: 60 years old and above 
 Pregnant: 10-14, 15-19 and 20-49 years old 
 
Specify the name of health facility and municipality/city and indicate the 
month/quarter/year. 
 
Column 1 Date of Consult ation. Write in this column the month, day and year 
the client was seen at the clinic for oral health care and services. 
 
Column 2 Family Serial Number.  Indicate in this column the number that 
corresponds to the number of the family folder or envelope or 
individual treatment record. This column will help you facilitat e retrieval 
of the client’s record during the return or follow-up visit. 
 
Column 3 Name of Client.  Write the complete name of the client (First Name, 
Middle Initial, Last Name) 
 
Column 4 Complete Address. Record the client’s permanent place of residence. 
This column will help you to monitor or follow-up the client. 
 
Column 5 Date of Birth. Write in this column the month, day and year of birth of 
the client. This column is important for the timeliness of other services 
to be provided. 
 
Column 6 Age/Risk Group. Write the age of the client at his/her last birthday 
under the proper category: 0-11 mos., 1 -4 y/o (12-59 mos.), 5 -9 y/o, 
10-14 y/o, 15-19 y/o, 20-59 y/o, ≥ 60 y/o and Pregnant.

F. Data Consolidation and Submission of Report to the Next 
Administrative Level 
F.1 Use of Summary Table for the Municipality/City on Oral Health 
The Summary Table on Oral Health is intended to record coverage of oral 
health status and oral health care and services among the newborn, infants, 
under-five children, school-aged children, adolescents, adults, senior citizens 
and pregnant women. 
Oral health status of children and provision of oral health care and services 
are consolidated by the nurse at the RHU/MHC level as basis in preparing the 
Quarterly Report to be submitted to the next level.  
Based on the data recorded in the Target Client List for Oral Health Care and 
Services, the nurse will consolidate the data using the Summary Table for the 
Municipality/City on Oral Health on a monthly basis. The Summary Table 
requires the disaggregation of the dat a by sex and by socio -economic status 
(NHTS or non -NHTS). Spaces for Quarterly subtotals are  provided including 
the annual Total at the end of the year.  
1. The first column  lists the Oral Health indicators to be tracked by the 
dental clinic/health facility; 
 
2. Place under the second column the targets of your health facility for 
each indicator at the start of the year. Consult the supervising nurse for 
the number to be entered under this column; 
 
3. Under each su cceeding monthly columns, record the number 
corresponding to each indicator for the month. These are segregated 
by sex; 
 
4. Under each quarter columns, write the total number of each target 
rendered with the services for the 3-month period. 
 
5. Provide the overall total  of each indicator  for the year at the last 
column. 
 
F.2 Quarterly Report on Oral Health (Q1) 
The data in the Summary Table are transferred by the nurse to the Q1 Report 
which is submitted to the PHO/HUC on a quarterly basis. Sa id reports are 
used by the P/CHO in preparing the report to be submitted to the DOH-CHD. 
 
The Quarterly Report on Oral Health Care and Services i s the official health 
report of the municipality/city for the quarter. The PHN forwards this report to 
the Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO every third week of the first 
month of the succeeding quarter  for provincial consolidation. The 
municipality/city prepares only one quarterly report. In case there is more than 
one RHU/MHC in the municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for 
directing the consolidation of all the quarterly data from different RHUs/MHCs 
and the preparation of one Quarterly Form for the municipality/city.

How to Accomplish the Quarterly Report on Oral Health 
 
1. Column 1 lists the indicators to be reported and is categorized into Total, NHTS 
and Non-NHTS. 
2. Under the Column 2, write the eligible population which will be used as 
denominator in computing the percentages/proportion. 
3. Columns 3 to 4 disaggregates the number of clients covered per indicator into 
male and female and 10-14 years old, 15-19 years old and 20-49 years old for 
pregnant women. 
4. Indicate under Column 5 the total and compute the proportion/percentage and 
write this under Column 6.

actions recommended under Column 8.

incorporated into the overall Quarterly Report to be submitted by the 
RHU to the higher administration together with the accomplishments 
of the other public health programs.
