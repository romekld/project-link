Chapter 4.3  
Child Care and Services 
 
 
A. Scope of Child Care and Services 
Child Care encompasses the package of health interventions and services that must 
be provided to the newborns, infants, under-five children, school-aged children, and 
children of older age groups (10-19 years old) including management of sick children. 
Some newborn care services however are reported as part of the Prenatal Care (e.g. 
Td immunization). The following enumerates the key child care and services that are 
tracked through the FHSIS. 
 
Newborn Care (0-28 days) 
 Immunization at birth (BCG, Hepatitis B within 24 hours) 
 Protection at birth from neonatal tetanus 
 Initiation of breastfeeding lasting for at least 90 minutes 
 
Infant Care (29 days-11 months old) 
 Immunization of BCG, DPT-H. influenzae type B-Hepatitis B dose 1, 2 and 
3, Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV) dose 1,2, and 3, Oral Polio 
Vaccine (OPV) dose 1, 2, and 3, Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV), Measles-
Containing Vaccine (MCV) at 9 months 
 Exclusive breastfeeding from birth up to 6 months of age 
 Complementary feeding initiation with continuous breastfeeding starting at 
6 months old 
 Low birth weight infants, ages 1-3 months, seen and given iron 
supplementation 
 Micronutrient Supplementation: Vitamin A and Micronutrient Powder 
(MNP) 
 
Under-five Children Care (1-4 years old) 
 Immunization of MCV at 12 months 
 Micronutrient Supplementation, Vitamin A and Micronutrient Powder 
(MNP) 
 Deworming, 2 doses, six months apart   
 
School-Aged Children (5-9 years old) 
 Deworming, 2 doses, six months apart 
 School-based Immunization of Td & MR (Grade 1) 
 
Adolescents (10-19 years old) 
 Deworming, 2 doses, six months apart 
 School-based Immunization of Td & MR (Grade 7 children)

adolescents is an essential process in the overall delivery of child  care and services. 
This serves as the main reference of the health care providers in prioritizing those to 
be visited and followed up, and to be provided with inf ormation/counseling and 
services. Proactively identifying and master listing of children is expected  to increase 
service coverage. These Master Lists if completely and exhaustively prepared and 
regularly updated will provide the entire population to be rea ched out and served.  
This M aster List is to be sourced from the completed and updated HH Profiles 
(Chapter 3) . The f ollowing are the steps in master listing the children  and 
adolescents. 
 
1. Based on the completed HH Profiles in each barangay (Chapter 3), the  BHS 
midwife with assistance from BHWs/NDPs/other volunteer workers shall 
summarize all those profiled belonging to the following age-groups:   
Part 1. List of Newborns (0-28 days old) 
Part 2. List of Infants (29 days-11 months old) 
Part 3. List of Under-five Children (1-4 years old) 
Part 4. List of School-Aged Children (5-9 years old) 
Part 5. List of Adolescents (10-19 years old)

Master List of Newborns, Infants, Under -five Children, School-aged Children and

- HH number/Family Serial Number 
- Name of Mother 
- Name of child/adolescent 
- Address of Mother and child/adolescent 
- Age and Birthday of each child/adolescent 
- Sex  
- Socio-Economic Status: NHTS member or Non-NHTS

3. The BHS midwife shall file and maintain th is Master List. This Master List will be 
useful as reference for Immunization, Nutrition, and Deworming. 
 
4. School-Based Immunization.  Note that there is a separate Master List to be 
prepared by the local health staff in connection with the provision of Td and MR to

are initially registered on the Individual Treatment Records (ITR s) used by the health

 Demographic Profile: Complete Name, Sex, Birthday, Age, Civil Status,

 Vital Signs:  BP, RR, etc. 
 Anthropometric Measurements: weight, height , mid-upper arm circumference 
(MUAC) 
 Chief Complaint  
 Diagnosis, management and treatment

especially if they have not designed or established their own: 
 
 ITR for Children and Adult 
 ITR for  Children to be Dewormed which can be used during the MDA or 
routine deworming in the health facility outside the MDA period 
 ITR for the Management of Sick Children categorized into 2: 
Form 1. Management of the Sick Young Infant Aged up to 2 months (before 
reaching 2 months old) 
Form 2. Management of the Sick Child age 2 months up to 5 years

1. Ensure that all items in the ITRs are filled -up upon interview or screening of 
the client; 
 
2. At the end of the day, collate all the accomplished ITRs as basis in 
accomplishing the Target Client List for Child Care; 
 
3. Maintain the accomplished ITRs and use the same to record the results of the 
follow-up visits of the client to the health facility.

F. Consolidating Child Care Coverage in each Barangay 
 
F.1 Using the Summary Table on Child Care at the Barangay Level 
 
The Summary Table on Child Care is intended to record coverage of immunization, 
nutrition and deworming services among the newborn, infants, under -five children, 
school-aged children and adolescents including care given to sick children. 
 
1. The first column lists the Child Care indicators to  be  tracked  by  your health 
facility; 
 
2. Place under the second column  the targets of your health facility for each 
indicator at the start of the year. Consult the supervising nurse for the number 
to be entered under this Column; 
 
3. Under each succeeding monthly columns, record the number corresponding to 
each indicator for the month. These are segregated by sex; 
 
4. Under each quarter columns, write the total number of each target rendered 
with the services for the 3-month period. 
  
5. Provide the overall total of each indicator for the year at the last column. 
 
F.2 Using the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT) at the Municipal/City 
Level 
 
The supervising nurse/FHSIS Coordinator in the C/MHO records all the child health 
care and service data into the Monthly Consolidation Table (MCT). This becomes the 
source document of the nurse in coming up with the quarterly report on Child Care at 
the municipal/city level.  
 
1. The first column  lists all the Child Care indicators being tracked by your 
health facility; 
 
2. The rest of the columns are intended for each of the barangays covered by 
the municipality/city. Write the name of each barangay per column; 
 
3. On a monthly basis, transfer the data pertinent to each Child Care indicator 
by barangay using the Monthly Report submitted by the Barangays  as the 
source;  
 
4. Use this to prepare the monthly and quarterly report to be submitted to the 
next administrative level.

G.1 Monthly Child Care Accomplishment Report (M1) 
 
The Monthly Program Accomplishment  Report is prepared by the midwife at the 
barangay level. The midwife fills up the Form to report the coverage of child care and 
services from the first day to the last day of the month. This is submitted to the nurse 
at the RHU/MHC for consolidation.   
 
How to Accomplish the Monthly Report on Child Care 
 
1. The M1 Report is divided into 4 Sections with corresponding indicators to be 
reported: 
 
Section C1. Coverage of Immunization Services 
Section C2. Coverage of Nutrition Services 
Section C3. Coverage of Deworming Services 
Section C4. Coverage of Management of Sick Infants and Children 
 
2. Column 1 lists the indicators to be reported and is segregated into total, NHTS and 
non-NHTS. 
 
3. Disaggregate the coverage into male and female under Columns 2 and 3 and write 
the total under Column 4.  
 
4. Indicate on the appropriate columns the coverage of the following child care 
services:  
 
Section C1.  Coverage of Immunization Services 
 
o Children Protected at Birth . Write on the space provided the total number of 
newborns whose mothers pregnant for the first time have received at least 2 
doses of TT/Td vaccination at least a month prior to delivery or mothers 
received at least 3 doses of TT/Td any time prior to the 2nd or more pregnancy. 
  
o Coverage of Immunization by antigen  Write on the space provided the total 
number of infants 0 -11 months who were given the specific antigen during the 
month. These include BCG, Hepatitis birth d ose within 24 hours after birth, 
DPT-HiB-HepB1, DPT-HiB-HepB2, DPT-HiB-HepB3, OPV1 , OPV 2,  OPV 3, 
IPV, PCV 1, PCV 2, PCV 3, MCV 1 at 9 months and MCV 2 at 12 months.  
 
o Fully Immunized Child . Write on the space provided the total number of 
children 0-11 months that have completed their immunization schedule during 
the month. To be fully immunized, the child must have been given BCG, 3 
doses of DPT-HiB-HepB, 3 doses of OPV, 1st dose of MCV vaccine at 9 
months, and 2nd dose at 12 months old.  The child is counted as FIC as soon as 
all the required vaccines are administered at 1 year of age.

G.2 Quarterly Program Accomplishment Report on Child Care (Q1) 
 
The Quarterly Report on Child Care and Services is the official health report of the 
municipality/city for the quarter.  It contains the consolidated three-month reports from 
all the BHS and RHU/MHC during the quarter . The PHN forwards this report to the 
Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO every third week of the first month of the 
succeeding quarter  for provincial consolidation. The m unicipality/city prepares only 
one quarterly report.  In case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the 
municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolidation of 
all the quarterly data from different RHUs/MHCs and the preparation o f one Quarterly 
Report for the municipality/city.  
 
How to Accomplish the Quarterly Report on Child Care. 
1. The Q1 Report is structured into 4 sections: 
Section C1. Coverage of Immunization Services 
Section C2. Coverage of Nutrition Services 
Section C3. Coverage of Deworming Services 
Section C4. Coverage of the Management of Sick Infants and Children 
2. Column 1 lists the indicators to be reported and is categorized into Total, NHTS 
and Non-NHTS. 
3. Under the Column 2, write the eligible population which will be used as 
denominator in computing the percentages/ proportion. 
4. Columns 3 to 4 disaggregates the number of children covered per indicator into 
male and female. 
5. Indicate under Column 5 the total and compute the proportion/percentage and 
write this under Column 6. 
6. Analyze the data on coverage and write your interpretation under Column 7 and 
actions recommended under Column 8. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
This Quarter Report on Child Care and Services shall be incorporated 
into the overall Quarterly Report to be submitted by the BHS/RHU to 
the higher administration together with the accomplishments of the 
other public health programs.
