Chapter 7 
Environmental Health and Sanitation Services 
 
 
A. Scope of Environmental Health and Sanitation Services 
 
The FHSIS continues to track and monitor the coverage of Environmental Health and 
Sanitation Services, some of which are enhanced in line with the commitment of the 
Philippine Government to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals by 2030. 
These services include: 
 
Access to Safe Water 
1.  access to basic safe water supply (Level I, II, III) 
2.  use of safely managed drinking-water services 
 
Access to Sanitation Services 
3. with basic sanitation facilities 
4. use of safely managed sanitation services 
5. certification of barangays as zero open defecation (ZOD) area 
 
Inspection and Issuance of Sanitary Permits 
6. Industrial Establishments 
 
 
B. Metadata 
The following outlines the key indicators to be tracked by the FHSIS on Environmental 
Health and Sanitation with their respective definitions, formula for computations, 
target, source of data and frequency of reporting.

C. Recording of Data 
 
The indicators on Environmental Health and Sanitation are not client-based but are 
households, institutions and barangay-based. In this regard, there is no Individual 
Treatment Record involved but rather a Master List of the following: 
 
1. Master List of Households on Environmental Health and Sanitation covering 
the following indicators: 
 
 Access to Basic Safe Water Supply 
 Use of Safely Managed Drinking-Water Services  
 Status of Sanitation Facility 
 Use of Safely Managed Sanitation Services  
 
How to Accomplish the Master List of Households on Environmental Health and 
Sanitation 
 
1.1 Based on the accomplished /updated Household Profile done every January 
and updated every first month of the succeeding quarters (Chapter 3), the 
Sanitary Inspectors with assistance from Sanitary Aides/BHWs shall 
copy/consolidate all the HHs that were profi led and transfer them into th is 
Master List of Households on Environmental Health and Sanitation; 
 
a. Indicate the barangay, municipality and  date the Master Listing is 
conducted 
 
b. Write the names of the HH heads under the first column by beginning with 
the  Family Name and then the First Name 
 
1.2 T he Master List of Households on Environmental Health and Sanitation is 
designed into 4 parts described as follows:  
 
Part 1. Household’s Access to Basic Safe Water Supply and Use of Safely 
Managed Drinking-Water Services 
 
Part 2. Household’s Status o n Sanitation Facility  and Use of Safely Managed 
Sanitation Services  
 
Part 3. Household’s Solid Waste Management 
 
Part 4. Household’s Status on Complete Sanitation Facilities  
 
1.3 The SI, with assistance from BHW s/other volunteers, undertakes HH visits and 
check HHs’ status on environmental health and sanitation preferably at one -
time visit.

E. Reporting of Program Accomplishment of the Environmental 
Health and Sanitation Program on a Monthly and Quarterly  
Basis 
 
The accomplishment s of the Environmental Health and Sanitation Program are 
submitted on a monthly and quarterly basis using the M1 and Q1 Report. 
 
E.1 Monthly Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage Report (M1) 
 
The Monthly NCD Program Accomplishment Report (M1) is prepared by at the  
barangay level. The form is filled -up to report the program accomplishments from the 
first day to the last day of the month. It is submitted to the nurse at the RHU/MHC for 
consolidation. 
 
Note that the M1 contains the accomplishments of all public health  programs being 
tracked through the FHSIS. The form is divided into sections where the various 
indicators per program are listed.   
 
E.2 Quarterly Program Accomplishment/Service Coverage Report (Q1) 
The Quarterly Program Accomplishment Report (Q1) is the official health report of the 
municipality/city for the quarter. It contains the consolidated three month reports from 
all the BHS and RHU/MHC during the quarter. The PHN forwards this report to the 
Provincial FHSIS Coordinator at the PHO every third week o f the first month of the 
succeeding quarter  for provincial consolidation. The municipality/city prepares only 
one quarterly report. In case there is more than one RHU/MHC in the 
municipality/city, the MHO/CHO shall be responsible for directing the consolid ation of 
all the quarterly data from different RHUs/MHCs and the preparation of one Quarterly 
Report for the municipality/city.  
 
The Q1 Report for Environmental Health and Sanitation Services includes all 
indicators for the quarter with columns on target,  total count and proportion or rate. It 
also provides columns for interpretation and recommendations.
