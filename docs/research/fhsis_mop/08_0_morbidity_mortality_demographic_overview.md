Chapter 8 
Recording and Reporting Morbidity, Mortality, 
Natality and Demographic Data 
 
In addition to the accomplishment  or service coverage of public health programs 
monitored and tracked by the FHSIS  as discussed in Chapters 4 to 7, the morbidity, 
mortality, natality  and selected demographic data are also recorded and reported 
through the FHSIS.  
 
Chapter 8 is structured into 3 parts described as follows: 
 
Part 1. Recording and Reporting Morbidity Data 
 
Part 2. Recording and Reporting Mortality and Natality Data 
 
Part 3. Recording and Reporting Demographic Data 
 
The following summarizes the key indicators to be monitored and tracked by the 
FHSIS: 
 
Morbidity Data 
 Morbidity Rate by type/group of disease, sex and age group 
 Ten Leading Causes of Morbidity by sex, age/vulnerable groups 
 
Mortality and Natality Data 
 Mortality Rate by sex 
 Maternal Mortality Ratio 
 Under-five Mortality Rate  
 Infant Mortality Rate 
 Neonatal Mortality Rate 
 Perinatal Mortality Rate (fetal and early neonatal deaths) 
 Livebirths 
 Adolescent Birth Rate 
 Crude Birth Rate 
 
Demographic Data 
 Population 
 No. of Barangays  
 No. of Households 
 No. of Health Centers: Municipal Health Centers, City Health Centers and 
Rural Health Units 
 No. of Barangay Health Stations 
 No. of local health personnel by category: physicians/doctors, dentists, nurses, 
midwives, medical technologists, nutritionist s/dietitians, sanitary engineer s, 
sanitary inspectors and active barangay health workers
