# ITR User Flow — Official FHSIS Reference

## Purpose

This document describes the Individual Treatment Record (ITR) as defined in the FHSIS Manual of Operations 2018, referenced by DOH Department Memorandum 2024-0007.

The ITR is the basic recording tool used by health facilities to capture client consultation data and clinical findings. It is not a separate reporting form; it feeds program-specific target client lists, registries, and downstream summary/reporting tools.

## Scope and Role Boundary

- Role context: health facility provider using the ITR at BHS / RHU / MHC / OPD level
- Official function: record consultation data, screening results, diagnosis, and treatment, then file the record for retrieval
- Not a standalone submission form: the ITR itself is maintained locally and used as the basis for TCLs, registries, and other program tools
- No independent ITR reporting deadline is defined in the FHSIS MOP; reporting deadlines apply to downstream forms such as M1, M2, Q1, and A1

## Canonical ITR Workflow

## 1) Register the Client

1. Client seeks consultation or avails of a service at the health facility.
2. Health provider opens the appropriate ITR.
3. The client is registered with basic demographic information.

Official ITR basic fields include:
- date of consult
- name
- address
- presenting symptoms or complaint
- screening results
- diagnosis
- treatment or management, if any

## 2) Record Clinical Details

1. Provider completes the ITR during interview, screening, examination, or consultation.
2. The ITR captures the appropriate service-specific information depending on the program.
3. The provider ensures all items in the applicable ITR are filled up.

General fields reflected in official ITR examples and program-specific variants include:
- complete name
- address
- age
- birthday
- sex
- vital signs such as BP, RR, temperature, heart rate, as applicable
- anthropometric measurements such as weight, height, MUAC, BMI, as applicable
- chief complaint
- diagnosis
- management and treatment

## 3) Use Program-Specific ITR Variants When Required

The FHSIS MOP states that some public health programs require more comprehensive assessment or consultation charts in addition to the general ITR.

Officially listed variants include:
- Family Planning Service Record (Form 1)
- Maternal Client Record for Prenatal Care
- Maternal Client Record for Post-Partum and Neonatal Care
- Children and Other Adults ITR
- Children to be Dewormed ITR
- Management of Sick Young Infants below 2 months
- Management of Sick Children 2 months to 5 years
- Dental Health Program ITR (Form 1)
- Filariasis ITR
- Schistosomiasis ITR
- Soil Transmitted Helminthiasis ITR
- Tuberculosis ITR
- Malaria ITR
- Leprosy ITR
- National Rabies Prevention and Control Program ITR
- PhilPEN CVD/NCD Risk Assessment Form
- CASDT forms for cervical and breast cancer screening
- Eye and Vision Assessment Form

## 4) File and Maintain the Record

1. After the consult, the accomplished ITR is filed and maintained by the health facility.
2. Records are kept in a systematic manner to facilitate retrieval for follow-up visits or consultations.
3. The same ITR is used to record follow-up results on later visits.

Official retention guidance from the MOP:
- ITRs are maintained by the health facility for follow-up retrieval.
- For oral health services, the chapter explicitly states the ITRs must be properly filed and maintained for five years.
- Other chapters repeat the same maintenance principle, but may not state a single universal retention period for all programs.

## 5) Collate for Downstream Tools

1. At the end of the day, accomplished ITRs are collated.
2. Collated ITR data is used as the basis for the appropriate Target Client List.
3. Program-specific TCLs and registries are updated from the ITR record.

Official downstream uses noted in the MOP:
- Family Planning: basis for TCL for FP services and Master List of WRA updates
- Maternal Care: basis for TCLs for prenatal, postpartum, and neonatal care
- Child Care: basis for TCL for child care services
- Oral Health: basis for TCL for oral health care and services
- Infectious Disease: basis for disease registries
- NCD: basis for NCD registries

## 6) Use in Program Chapters

The ITR serves as the common recording layer across major FHSIS service clusters:

- Chapter 4.1 Family Planning Services
- Chapter 4.2 Maternal Care and Services
- Chapter 4.3 Child Care and Services
- Chapter 4.4 Oral Health Care and Services
- Chapter 5 Infectious Disease Prevention and Control Services
- Chapter 6 Non-Communicable Disease Prevention and Control Services

## Reporting Deadlines

> **Source:** FHSIS Manual of Operations 2018, Chapter 2, Table 2 and related program chapters.

### Reports Owned by This Tool

| Report | Form | Frequency | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| None | ITR is a recording tool, not a reporting form | On consult / follow-up | No standalone deadline in the MOP |

### Deadline Notes

- The ITR itself is maintained locally at the health facility.
- Official submission deadlines apply to downstream forms such as M1, M2, Q1, and A1.
- The ITR supports those reports indirectly through TCL, registry, and summary table updates.

## Official FHSIS Notes

- The ITR is the basic consultation record used by health facilities.
- There is no standard ITR format across all facilities; design, content, and size may vary.
- The record must capture enough information to support follow-up care and downstream FHSIS tools.
- The ITR is not the same as the TCL, Summary Table, or monthly/quarterly reporting forms.
